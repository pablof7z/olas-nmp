# Episode: Pivot from NIP-29 communities to photo-feed-first client

## Prior State

Olas was conceived as a private-first Instagram for small communities (families, closed groups) using NIP-29 relay-based communities and potentially MLS for encryption. The main feed would show posts from all groups a user belongs to, with per-group views. Five parallel Opus agents were dispatched to generate product questions around community invites, privacy, group lifecycle, and competitive positioning.

## Trigger

User explicitly pivoted: 'Ok, let's pivot for now: let's just build a good pictures-feed-first client (which is what Olas has been from the beginning in its previous incarnations) we'll add NIP-29 stuff later.'

## Decision

Olas v1 is a photo-feed-first Nostr client with WoT filtering as the primary spam defense. NIP-29 community/group features are explicitly deferred. The full product spec (features, flows, WoT, media, visual system, motion) was built around this direction.

## Consequences

- NIP-29 community features and MLS encryption are out of scope for v1
- WoT becomes the central trust mechanism (spam filter for Network feed) rather than a community-membership mechanism
- Product spec centers on: Following/Network dual feeds, magazine-style per-relay views, image filters at publish, Blossom media servers with configurable defaults
- The 5-agent product-question output was superseded by the pivot; a fresh spec was generated by parallel Opus+Codex brainstorm
- Empty-feed problem solved by defaulting new users to Network (WoT-filtered global) until they follow ≥15 accounts, then auto-switching to Follows

## Open Tail

- NIP-29 community features remain planned but undated
- MLS end-to-end encryption deferred along with NIP-29
